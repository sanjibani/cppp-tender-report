The standout pattern: IndianOil refineries dominate the high-risk list

Six IndianOil refineries appear in the top 25 composite-risk orgs (Panipat, Paradip, Guwahati, Gujarat, Barauni, Digboi). Combined with elevated threshold-engineering rates (5-19% vs national 1.1%), this points to a systemic procurement pattern across IndianOil's downstream operations.

The second pattern: PSU vs PSU divergence

PSUs are NOT homogeneous. BHEL Bhopal single-bid share rose from 33% to 90% in 6 years — a structural deterioration. ISRO and MOIL show structural high single-bid (qualified-vendor pool, defensible). The difference is the TREND direction, not the absolute level.

Three university/education orgs flagged

BHU (Banaras Hindu University) at 16.83% threshold-engineering — the second-highest rate in the entire dataset. IIT Roorkee, Delhi University VPCI, and a handful of other educational institutions show elevated single-bid or threshold patterns. These warrant specific scrutiny because academic procurement is supposed to follow standard competitive norms.

7Methodology and Caveats

What each signal measures

1. Single-bid % — Share of contracts awarded with only one bidder. World Bank research (Kenny & Musatova, 2020) identifies single-bid as the single strongest statistical corruption indicator. Caveats: structural for PSUs with captive vendor pools.

2. Just-below-threshold engineering — Share of contracts valued in the band 0-5% above each major Indian audit threshold (50L, 2Cr, 5Cr, 10Cr). Manipulation indicator per CAG audit observations. Caveats: legitimate variance around round thresholds is normal; we look for disproportionate clustering.

3. Round-number % — Share of contracts valued at exact multiples of ₹1 lakh or larger. Legitimate negotiated values rarely land on exact round numbers; suspicious concentration suggests estimation manipulation.

4. HHI (vendor concentration) — Herfindahl-Hirschman Index. Sum of squared market shares by vendor. >0.25 = high concentration. Caveats: structural for narrow-vendor-pool departments.

5. Trend direction — Change in single-bid % between 2018-19 and 2024-25. Catches departments where procurement quality is actively deteriorating.

What we cannot conclude from this data alone

  • Individual officer names involved in award decisions (CPPP does not publish decision-maker names)
  • Quid-pro-quo / kickback patterns (would require RTI/CAG/CBI cross-reference)
  • Shell-company director networks (would require MCA company master data)
  • Vendor financial health (would require MCA + GSTIN + bank data)

What this analysis can defensibly claim

  • That certain procurement patterns (multi-flag risk) are statistically over-represented at specific organisations
  • That these patterns warrant journalistic investigation
  • That multi-flag risk combined with deterioration over time is the strongest signal available from public data

Data limitations

  • Winner addresses are only 11% populated in CPPP and mostly just city+pincode — limits shell-company clustering to coarse analysis
  • Published dates only 38% populated — limits bid-window × corrigendum intersection analysis
  • Contract values are sometimes "0" — likely rate contracts where final PO value isn't published
  • Vendor name spelling is inconsistent — same company appears under multiple spellings (Larsen and Toubro Limited / LARSEN AND TOUBRO LIMITED) inflating apparent vendor count

What to do with this list

For the top 10-20 organisations on the composite risk list, the recommended follow-up workflow is:

  1. Pull that organisation's contracts from the public CPPP data
  2. Cross-reference vendor names against MCA (Ministry of Corporate Affairs) company master to find common directors
  3. Search CBI / state ACB press releases for any existing cases involving those vendors or organisations
  4. File RTI applications for the specific procurement files of the flagged contracts
  5. Cross-reference with CAG audit paras (annual reports published online)

The strongest publications will pair a multi-flag statistical finding (this analysis) with one specific adjudicated or RTI-confirmed case — that's how statistical anomaly becomes a story.
